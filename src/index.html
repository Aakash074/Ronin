Who We Are

US National Champions!

AeroFlow Racing was a team of five seniors (left to right: Arul Gupta, Ayush Goyal, Michael Jin, Shivam Patel, and Poojan Mehta) from the Wheeler Center for Advanced Studies in Marietta, GA that competed in the 2018 World Finals in Singapore. Their team had a drive to learn from their mistakes, improve their team unity, and progress to achieve the impossible. Through their experience at the 2018 United States National Competition, they built a multitude of connections related to various aspects of the competition, developed a vast platform for communication and team branding, and understood the actions necessary to succeed at the world finals. AeroFlow Racing: one team, with one goal, with one passion.

OUR TEAM

Poojan Mehta

Design Engineer

Poojan is responsible for designing a car that can achieve flawless motion. He works within the heart of the competition and does so effectively using Autodesk Fusion, mentorship from outside sources, and knowledge gained from previous cars and physics classes. From all this, Poojan has a strong grasp of the tools he must use, and the aerodynamics principles he must apply.

Arul Gupta

Executive Manager & Marketing Director

Arul is in charge of keeping the team organized, efficient, and productive to ensure success. This includes the introduction of time and project management tools to convey the schedule and roles that must be followed. He is also tasked with raising funds and building connections to attain the team’s financial goals.

Shivam Patel

Resource & Outreach Director

Shivam acquires and keeps track of the resources necessary for the success of the team. This is critical in ensuring that we do not fall behind schedule and that work can be completed productively. In addition, Shivam sets up outreach event, so that we are able to raise awareness for both our team as well as the F1 in Schools competition.

Ayush Goyal

Graphic Designer & Testing Engineer

As graphic designer, Ayush establishes the unique and innovative branding of our team by leading all design needs from the pit display to the website. He possesses a wide range of abilities as evident by his ability to design, code, and test simulations of the car through CFD. Ayush also calculates airflow data and provides it to Poojan in order to progress the car design.

Michael Jin

Manufacturing Engineer

Michael manufacturers our team’s car using a CNC mill after it has been designed in CAD. He works efficiently and carefully to create a high quality physical car that can perform at a high level. Additionally, he has thorough knowledge of the car design which helps ensure the car is fully ready to test after manufacturing.

THE CHALLENGE

  • 20,000,000 Students
  • 40 Countries
  • 1 Experience

F1 in Schools is a worldwide competition consisting of several teams made up of kids aged 9-19. In this vastly competitive league, teams of 3-6 develop cars utilizing CAD and other related tools. However, the complexity of what we do reaches beyond mere engineering and into fields of business/marketing, graphic design, and possibly most importantly if considering real-world application, team management and cooperation. Then, each of these skill sets developed throughout the team process is put to the test at regional, national, and world level competitions. As a result, F1 in Schools has created a system which effectively develops and enhances students’ learning experience through their interests as well as by teaching necessary real-world situations/applications.

PAST RESULTS

At the 2018 World Finals, AeroFlow Racing had an amazing experience. Over the course of 10 days, they had the opportunity to meet with other bright students from around the world and explore Singapore. In the end, they had the second fastest car, and placed 15th in the World.

At the 2018 US National Finals in Austin, AeroFlow Racing once again had a memorable experience. Along with placing first overall, they received the Best Engineered Car award! Above all, they learned an immense amount to ensure their ability to compete successfully in the World Competition.
